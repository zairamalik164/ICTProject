How your mental health is imporatnt for you?

Mental health is vital for over-all well-being, influencing how we think, feel and act in daily life. It affects relationships, decision-making and how we handle stress. Prioritizing mental health fosters resilience, productivity and a balanced life. Just as physical health requires care, mental health needs attention through self-care, seeking help when needed and reducing stigma around mental illness. A healthy mind ensures a healthier, happier life.

Mental Vulnerabilities

Mental Health Vulnerabilities are the factors that increase the risk of developing mental health challenges or disorders.

  1. Genetic Vulnerabilities

    30%-50% increased risk of developing same condition as that of a close family member is suffering from.

  2. Early Childhood Adversity

    Individuals with 4 or more ACEs are 4.6 times more likely to experience depression and 12 times morelikely to attempt suicide.

  3. Trauma and Stress

    About 70% of adults experience at least one traumatic event in their lifetime. PTSD develops in 20% of them.
    Nearly 40%-50% professionals are reported with the symptoms of anxiety or depression.

  4. Social Isolation

    Lonely individuals are 30% more likely to develop depression.
    1 in 3 adults globally reports feelings of loneliness.

  5. Economic and Environmental Factors

    People living in poverty are 3 times more likelyto develop mental health disorders.
    Unemployed individuals have a 34%-50% higher risk of experiencing depression and anxiety.

  6. Chronic Physical Illness

    People with chronic illnesses 25%-50% risk of developing depression or anxiety.

  7. Biological Factors

    10%-20% of women are affected with postpartum depression.
    20% of women are affected with meno-pause related depression.

  8. Substance Use

    50%-60% of individuals with alcohol dependence have at least one co-occurring mental disorder.

  9. Sociocultural Factors

    Marginalized groups are 2-3 times more likely to experiencemental health issues due to discrimination.

  10. neurodevelopmental Vulnerabilities

    ADHD affects about 5%-8% of people globally.
    Approximately 40%-70% of idividuals with autism experience co-occurring mental health conditions.
